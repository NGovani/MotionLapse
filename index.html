About Us

Getting Into Photography

Photography is one of the simplest methods for a person to express themselves artistically. All that's neccessary is a phone and an imaginative eye, but once an amateur wishes to develop their skills further with more advanced tools the barrier becoming exceedingly more expensive.

Fairness

Here at MotionLapse we want to strive for equality and give people who venture into the world of Photography a fair chance at being the best they can be. This foundatinal principle of accessibilty runs throughout the design of our product.

Our Product

MotionLapse is...

Portable

When you're out trying to capture your next magnum opus you don't need wire and heavy electronics bogging you down, so MotionLapse is designed to be lightweight and portable to keep up with you.

Affordable

Making great Photography shouldn't break your bank, so don't let it. MotionLapse allows you to take the pictures you want and let's you still pay rent.

Customisable

Affordability doesn't mean non-versatile, we believe that you're at your best when you're making the decisions. That's why we've designed MotionLapse to let you choose what kind of pictures you take.

How to setup a Website

  1. Sign up for GitHub
  2. Go to https://pages.github.com/
  3. Follow the steps to intiialise your website and it will be given a preset Domain
  4. If you'd like a custom domain go to the settings in the Github repository and enter your own URL into the custom domain field
    1. This will make a CNAME file in your repository, do no delete
    2. Now go to your DNS provider account. This is where you bought your domain. If you haven't, go buy a domain a custom one ain't free. Just use GoDaddy.com
    3. In here create a CNAME file that points to your Github pages URL. This will likely be the {YOUR_USERNMAE_HERE}.github.io/{YOUR_REPO_NAME_HERE}

Websites & GDPR

The purpose of a product website is to present information to the viewer, therefore images and iconography are essential for keeping the user interested and for controlling where they look on the screen. You also have to keep information concise and relevant to that user. The website made for this coursework does not actually collect any user data therefore isn't really subject to GDPR. If in the future this website was to become real, a forumn site would likely be used alongside a webportal and in both cases privacy would need to be maintained. To get people onto the website itself a combination paying ad services such as google to target consumers with ads of the product and maintaing a social media presence.
